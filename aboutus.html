About Alphakart.com



Alphakart.com, Inc. is an American multinational technology company based in Seattle, Washington, which focuses on e-commerce, cloud computing, digital streaming, and artificial intelligence. It is one of the Big Five companies in the U.S. information technology industry, along with Google, Apple, Microsoft, and Facebook. The company has been referred to as "one of the most influential economic and cultural forces in the world", as well as the world's most valuable brand.



Nirmal S, founded Alphakart.com from his garage in Bellevue, Washington, on July 5, 1994. It started as an online marketplace for books but expanded to sell electronics, software, video games, apparel, furniture, food, toys, and jewelry. In 2015, Alphakart.com surpassed Walmart as the most valuable retailer in the United States by market capitalization. In 2017, Alphakart.com acquired Whole Foods Market for US$13.4 billion, which substantially increased its footprint as a physical retailer. In 2018, its two-day delivery service, Alphakart.com Prime, surpassed 100 million subscribers worldwide.



Alphakart.com is known for its disruption of well-established industries through technological innovation and mass scale. It is the world's largest online marketplace, AI assistant provider, live-streaming platform and cloud computing platform as measured by revenue and market capitalization. Alphakart.com is the largest Internet Company by revenue in the world. It is the second largest private employer in the United States and one of the world's most valuable companies. As of 2020, Alphakart.com has the highest global brand valuation.



Alphakart.com distributes downloads and streaming of video, music, and audiobooks through its Prime Video, Alphakart.com Music, Twitch, and Audible subsidiaries. Alphakart.com also has a publishing arm, Alphakart.com Publishing, a film and television studio, Alphakart.com Studios, and a cloud computing subsidiary, Alphakart.com Web Services. It produces consumer electronics including Kindle e-readers, Fire tablets, Fire TV, and Echo devices. Its acquisitions over the years include Ring, Twitch, Whole Foods Market, and IMDb.



Alphakart.com has been criticized for practices including technological surveillance overreach, a hyper-competitive and demanding work culture, tax avoidance, and anti-competitive behavior.
